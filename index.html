Live Lamp

How could we display live data in way that is meaningless to the uninitiated, but immediately accessible to those who are in-the-know?

How about a lamp? A color-changing lamp. Lamps are everywhere.

The changes in color would seem decorative to most, but could clearly relay critical information to those looking for it.

Who would need this? How about a student and a teacher in a classroom? Students with emotional disorders often develop secret signals that allow the student to inform the teacher that she is getting stressed and might not be able to handle it. What if the lamp were connected to the student's FitBit, and communicated the sudden rise in her heart rate before she even realized it herself?

I'm working on a web-connected RGB lamp to use for this purpose. It uses a BeagleBone Black, which is fun. There will be a full write-up here, but for now you can track my progress on MIT's awesome site BuildInProgress.

Netcar

Walgreens has the best RC car. For like $15 you can get the Thunder Tumbler (or whatever they may have renamed it to this year). Is it fun to use as an RC car? I have no idea. It's awesome because of how easy it is to hack (see this tutorial) . Seriously, the hardest parts of making an RC car are making the stupid H-bridges and figuring out a nice chassis. With the Thunder Tumbler, both are done! You really just touch the right pad with 5V, and watch the wheel spin.

If you're lucky, you'll end up with one of the old models that has through-hole parts; otherwise, you'll have to be careful not to rip off the pads when you remove the surface-mount IC.

I hooked up a Raspberry Pi to it and wrote a simple PWM motor controller, then my brother wrote a simple HTTP interface to it. Then he wrote a PyGame app for the PC to drive it with. All this code is available on my GitHub.

My friend worked at the Microsoft store, so he 3D-printed the cool blue case for me.

Ring

Molly always used to wear a Celtic-braid ring that her dad gave her. If I expected her to put on mine instead, I needed a worthy replacement.

After going to a couple different jewellery shops who didn't seem to understand the word custom, I found J. West Designs. He and I went back and forth on the design a dozen times and burned through two wax models until I earned the title "Most Particular Customer" and he created a gorgeous ring.

I have the only wax model, so don't think you'll get yourself one!

It took me about an hour of playing with my grapher and studying the photo of her old one to figure out that there are four strands, not three.

There are lots of options for how the strands could come up to hold the diamond. We ended up doing option A. The other two strands keep going and cross underneath the diamond.
